    Research outline

    My research is in the field of gravitational wave astrophysics. My supervisors are Paul Lasky and Eric Thrane. I’m a part of the OzGrav organisation and a member of the LIGO Scientific Collaboration. I’m excited about using gravitational waves to study parts of the universe that we can’t see with electromagnetic observations.

    I work primarily on things to do with dynamical formation of binary black holes. Particularly, I’m interested in binary formation in globular clusters.

    We can tell that binaries form in dense environments by the eccentricity encoded in the gravitational waves that we detect from their mergers. This video is a great illustration of a three-body interaction cusing a highly eccentric binary.

    In other projects, I’m involved in developing bilby, a modular Python library facilitating Bayesian inference, optimised for gravitational wave science.

    Before my adventures down under, I completed my MSci in Physics at Birmingham University in the UK. I did my Master’s project with Andreas Freise, using Python code to model the noise at gravitaitonal wave detectors. I helped to develop Space Py Quest, a Python code that lets you simulate your own gravitational wave detections! Its more glamorous older sister is Space Time Quest, a game with lovely graphics that lets you build and run your own detector without touching the code.

    During my time at the University of Birmingham, I was lucky enough to work with Ilya Mandel as a summer student. I worked on comparing different methods of evolving virtual stars within COMPAS, a code that simulates and analyses stellar evolution in order to work out how LIGO’s observed binaries formed.
